How to Get There

Travel Options

Transport Options

Fly:

Cork airport is approx 35 minutes from Ballyvolane House.

Direct flights from London to Cork are Operated by Aer Lingus and Ryanair from most London Airports.

Direct flights from London to Dublin are more frequent and often cheaper, however the drive from Dublin Airport takes 2.5 hours.

Drive:

You will need some form of transport to get around while you’re here, please specify your preferences when you RSVP so we can organise car-pools.

Cars can be rented from Cork Airport and Dublin Airport. We have a discount online with www.holidayautos.co.uk (Promo code: Anna_Chris_Wedding_2016).

Ferries run from Holyhead to Rosslare which is 2.5 hours from the venue if you’re thinking of making the drive over.

Taxi:

Fermoy Taxis: +353 25 34477

Midleton Cabs: +353 21 461 3789

Cork Taxi Co-op: +353 21 427 2222

Taxis are difficult to get late in the evening. We will be arranging buses to take everyone back to the accommodation listed below. It is possible to leave cars in Ballyvolane until Sunday.

Parking at the Church:

There is minimal parking available in the church grounds, however you should be able to park in the Supervalu carpark or on the quay nearby.

Explore Co. Cork

Local Attractions and Curiose...

East Cork (Around the Venue):
Food:

Ballymaloe House - Famous cookery school

Longueville House - Country estate and restaurant

Midelton Farmers Market - (Sat morning) Sure, it’s in the Super Valu carpark, but good for food on the go

Pat Shortt's Bar - You might find yourself there on Friday night!

Attractions:

Jameson Brewery - Tours, tasting and food

Youghal - Pronounced Yawl

Cobh - Cathedral, Titanic Museum

Fota - Fota house, gardens, wildlife park

Stephen Pearce Pottery - We've all seen Ghost...

Cork City:
Food:

English Market - Great food and delicatessen

Cafe Paradiso - A vegetarian restaurant, where no one will judge you

Crawford Gallery Cafe - A Friendly informal lunch place

Hayfield Manor - 5 Star Hotel

The Barn - Fine dining at a reasonable price

Pubs:

Sin é - The self-styled home of Trad Music

The Franciscan Well - Beer brewery dating back to 1219

Mutton Lane - A Cork pub much admir'd

Attractions:

Shandon Bells - Climb the tower and ring the (wedding) bells

Crawford Art Gallery - Seek out a classic Louis le Brocquy

Glucksman Gallery - University College Cork's gallery

Kinsale:
Kinsale - A beautiful seaside town 20 mins from Cork Airport. Great for exploring and sea walks with lots of places to eat
Food:

Fishy Fishy - A favourite... Try the fish!

The Bullman - After a walk around Charles Fort

The Spaniard - An Olde Pub

Perryville House - A 4 Star guesthouse

Man Fridays - Long running restaurant

Beaches/Cliff Walks:

Sandy Cove - Small sheltered beach

Charles Fort - Old military fort, museum and walk

Garrylucas - A blue flag beach, great for a windy walk

Nohoval Cove - Best cliff walks going, and bracing swim off the rocks

Blarney:
Blarney - Seaside town 20 mins from Cork Airport great for a day of exploring and see walks with lots of good places to eat.

Blarney Castle - for the obligatory kissing of the Blarney Stone

Blarney Woolen Mills - Everyone needs an Aran Sweater

There's lots more to see and do in West Cork, if you have more time, let us know and we can make some recommendations!
